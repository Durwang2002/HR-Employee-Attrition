# 📊 HR Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the most critical challenges faced by organizations. High employee turnover increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, and impacts overall business performance.

This project analyzes the **HR Employee Attrition Dataset** using Python to identify the major factors contributing to employee attrition and provides actionable business recommendations for improving employee retention.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

The HR department wants to answer the following business questions:

* Why are employees leaving the company?
* Which departments have the highest attrition?
* Does overtime increase employee turnover?
* Does salary affect employee retention?
* Which employee segments are at higher risk?
* What business actions can reduce attrition?

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition patterns.
* Perform data cleaning and preprocessing.
* Conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA).
* Identify key business drivers of attrition.
* Validate findings using statistical testing.
* Generate business insights and recommendations.

---

# 📂 Dataset Information

* **Dataset:** IBM HR Employee Attrition
* **Records:** 1,470 Employees
* **Features:** 35 Columns (after feature engineering)
* **Target Variable:** Attrition

# 📈 Key Business Insights

* Employee attrition is influenced by multiple business factors rather than a single variable.
* Overtime employees show higher attrition compared to employees without overtime.
* Lower job satisfaction is associated with increased employee turnover.
* Poor work-life balance contributes to employee attrition.
* Some departments experience significantly higher attrition than others.
* Certain job roles contribute disproportionately to employee exits.
* Lower salary groups generally have higher attrition rates.
* Younger employees are more likely to leave the organization.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime through workload balancing.
* Improve employee engagement and job satisfaction programs.
* Review compensation strategies for high-risk employee groups.
* Provide structured career growth and promotion opportunities.
* Develop department-specific retention strategies.
* Monitor high-risk employee segments using HR dashboards.
